This project began as a "speed" reading trainer. After seeing an RSVP implementation on social media, it felt like a simple way to explore the new capabilities of coding agents. That was trivial even back in December/January, so I branched out into other areas of speed and comprehension "training," based on my understanding of the current research and personal experience. It has since transitioned into more or less an eBook reader, with additional functionality to allow more streamlined speed and comprehension work if desired. Various visuals can help the reader practice reading at specific words-per-minute rates, or introduce extra difficulty (some research suggests minor gains from generation effects by dropping context -- for example, when one is *re-reading* a passage, it may be better for learning to require some recall/generation rather than pure recognition). There are tools for memorizing texts verbatim, if that interests you; as well as LLM-generated (currently via Gemini API) comprehension checks (~8 questions) and exams (with configurable difficulty). Questions from the comprehension checks are added to a light-weight Leitner SRS system to review for longer-term retention. Mistakes go into Box 1. Correct answers are placed in Box 2.
